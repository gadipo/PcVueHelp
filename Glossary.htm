Glossary of 3D Terms Used in the HMI

3D model

In the HMI, a '3D model' means a DirectX (.x) file. You can use 3D packages and tools to convert models and drawings from other formats to DirectX.

Alpha channel

A means of blending a degree of transparency with colors, e.g. for water, glass, smoke/fog/mist and surface film effects.

Ambient color

Defines the color of the light reflected off a lighted object's surface.

Azimuth

Rotation angle of the camera around its Y axis. For example, if the camera were a person's head, the azimuth angle would be the rotation angle of the head to the left or right.

Backface culling

A technique to save system resources by only rendering the surfaces (polygons) that are facing toward the camera.

Built-in camera model

A 3D camera model provided by the HMI.

Camera

A point of view onto a 3D window.

Camera model

A 3D model that provides pre-configured parameters for a window camera.

Custom camera model

Any of the 3D camera models located in the project libraries.

Design material

A material used to render an object in a window in Design mode.

Diffuse color

In principle, this is the color of the light reflection on an object under pure white light. It is usually defined as the color of the object itself rather than a reflection.

Distance to target

Distance between the camera (or eye) and the target.

Emissive color

Similar to the ambient color except that the object's surface need not be lit.

Eye

Same as Camera.

Frame

The hierarchy of components of an object. This has nothing to do with 'frame' in the context of visual animation, e.g. as in an animated GIF file.

Gizmo

A tool for rotating and scaling a 3D model in Draw mode.

Indexed material

Material specified in the header of the model file.

Local material

Material that is part of a mesh's attributes.

Material

The visual representation of the surface of an object.

Mesh

The surface attributes of a 3D object.

Orthographic

Describes a 2D projection of a 3D model without perspective. Parallel lines look parallel and lines of the same length appear with the same length.

Pitch

Rotation angle of the camera around its X axis. For example, if the camera were a person's head, the pitch angle would be the rotation angle of the head looking up or down.

Pivot

The 'center' of an object, where the Gizmo tool originates.

Roll

Rotation angle of the camera around its Z axis.

Specular color

Defines the highlights on an object, so as to control an object's glossiness and shininess. It is usually set to a brighter shade of the diffuse color. See also: Specular level.

Specular level

Adjusts the sharpness of the highlights.

Surface geometry

A collection of faces (polygons) bound together in a particular manner to define the outside of a 3D object.

Target

The focus point of a camera in the 3D world. Each target axis is always parallel with the corresponding axis in the 3D world.

Yaw

Rotation angle of the camera around its Y axis.
