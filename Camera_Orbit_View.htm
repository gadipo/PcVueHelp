3D Camera Orbit View

The main purpose of the Orbit View mode is to make a window camera orbit around a target. To do so you hold down the right-hand mouse button and move the mouse.

Mouse movement

Result

Sideways

The camera orbits around the target Y-axis while always keeping the same target. This changes the camera position and azimuth.

Forward and backward

The camera orbits around the target X-axis while always keeping the same target. This changes the camera position and pitch.

Other mouse or keyboard actions can be used to move the camera as follows.

Mouse/keyboard action

Result

Hold down the mouse's central button then move the mouse sideways or forward and backward.

Moves the camera so as to reduce or increase the distance between the camera and the target. The camera moves toward or away from the target as you move the mouse to the left (or away) or to the right (or toward you) respectively.

Roll the mouse's wheel

As above.

Use the Left or Right Arrow key

Moves the camera horizontally to left or right respectively. This changes the camera target.

Use the Up or Down Arrow key

Moves the camera forward or backward respectively. This changes the camera target.

Use the Page Up or Page Down key

Moves the camera along the 3D world Y-axis. This changes the camera target.
