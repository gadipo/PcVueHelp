Overview of the Navigator

The Navigator is a tool for visualizing an entire mimic as a thumbnail picture. A black rectangle, called the viewfinder, shows the portion of the mimic that is currently displayed in the mimic window. The navigator is available in two formats.

  • As an independent window in which the active mimic window (the one that has the focus) is represented. This is known as the Main Navigator. Show picture
  • As a tool embedded in the mimic which it represents. This is called the Mimic Navigator. Show picture

The navigator is not available for 3D mimic windows.

The thumbnail picture

The thumbnail picture is a screen capture of the content of the mimic window and is updated when the content is modified or when resizing the navigator. The screen capture is of the mimic window in design mode and therefore design changes such as add, remove, size or change the properties of an object will update the thumbnail. Runtime changes, such as those caused by animation, do not update the thumbnail.

The thumbnail picture always has the same aspectratio as that of the mimic and is delimited by light gray bounding box.

The viewfinder

The viewfinder is a black rectangle on top of the thumbnail picture indicating the area of the mimic that is currently visible in the mimic window.

If the mimic window has scrollbars, the user can move the viewfinder to the desired area of the thumbnail and so display, in the mimic window, the corresponding area of the mimic.
