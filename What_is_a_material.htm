What is a 3D Material?

A material is a collection of properties that define how the surface of a mesh is represented visually. A material is composed of the following properties. The HMI column indicates whether the property is available in the HMI's 3D Properties dialog.

Property

HMI

Description

Diffuse color

Yes

The diffuse color is the color of the light reflected from a surface under pure white light, but it is usually defined as the color of the object itself.

Specular color

Yes

The specular color is used for the highlights. Together with the specular level, it controls the shininess of a surface. The specular color is usually configured with a brighter version of the diffuse color. A black specular color will make a surface appear matt.

Specular level

Yes

The specular level controls the sharpness of the highlights. A larger value will contract the highlight areas and render the surface shinier. Smaller values stretch the highlight areas and render the surface duller. If the specular color is black, the specular level is unused.

Ambient color

No

The color of the light reflected from a surface that is lighted.

Emissive color

Yes

The emissive color is similar to the ambient color except that the surface does not require any light. In a scene with no light, a surface defined with an emissive color other than black can be seen. In a scene with light, it is usually applied so as to adjust the overall brightness of an object surface.

Opacity

Yes

Controls the transparency of the surface. Also known as Alpha.

Texture

No

A texture is an image mapped onto the surface. The final visual representation depends on the surface material attributes (colors and opacity) and the lighting.

Diffuse, specular and emissive colors are defined in the HMI as RGB colors (red-green-blue).

Local and Indexed materials

In a 3D model, there can be two types of material, according to where its properties are declared.

  • Local - The properties of the material are configured with, and only available to, a particular mesh.
  • Indexed - The properties of the material are configured as part of the model and are available to any mesh in the model.

A mesh can use a local or indexed material, or both. When using an indexed material, the mesh references the material's name.
