3D Coordinate System

Typically 3D graphics applications use two types of Cartesian coordinate systems: left-handed and right-handed. In both coordinate systems, the positive X-axis points to the right and the positive Y-axis points up. You can remember which direction the positive Z-axis points by pointing the fingers of either your left or right hand in the positive X-direction and curling them into the positive Y-direction. The direction your thumb points, either toward or away from you, is the direction that the positive Z-axis points for that coordinate system.
