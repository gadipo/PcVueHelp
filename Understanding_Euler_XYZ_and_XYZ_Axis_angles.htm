Understanding Euler XYZ and XYZ Axis Angles

The HMI supports two different methods for specifying the orientation of 3D models and frames

  • Euler XYZ angles
  • Rotational vectors

About Euler XYZ angles

This method is the easiest to understand but has major limitations and is not contiguous. It is described by a sequence of three rotations in the X, Y and Z axes. The axes are sometimes referred to as yaw, pitch and roll. The Euler method has the following disadvantages.

  • The order in which the rotations are executed affects the final orientation. In the HMI, the order in which rotations are executed is XYZ.
  • If angles of more than 90 degrees are used the object may suddenly flip because an axis becomes switched.
  • It is possible for two axis to temporarily line up creating a condition known as Gimbal Lock. As a result, some combinations of angle are impossible to resolve.

About rotational vectors (XYZ Axis Angle)

This is the most universal and best representation of a 3D angle although sometimes difficult to visualize. The orientation is represented by an axis described as a 3D vector (X, Y and Z) and an angle about that axis.

Changing from Euler XYZ angles to rotational vectors (XYZ Axis Angle)

Changing from the Euler representation to XYZ Axis Angle in the 3D Properties dialog always works, but the opposite is not true and may lead to some approximation errors.
