Interpreting 21 CFR Part 11 Requirements in PcVue 

The topics in this book provide guidelines to interpreting the requirements of 21 CFR Part 11 in terms of PcVue features. However, they do not infer a commitment on behalf of the developers of PcVue in any way or form. Ultimately, it is the responsibility of the persons or company developing the application to ensure that the 21 CFR Part 11 requirements are met.

In this text paragraphs from 21 CFR Part 11 are in an italic typeface followed by the interpretation of the requirements in PcVue in a normal type face.

Paragraphs from 21 CFR Part 11 that are not considered to have an impact on the configuration of PcVue are not included in the topics of this book. However, these paragraphs may have an impact on the overall system design and operational procedures and must not be ignored.

The developer must be aware that using these features is not a guarantee of compliance, in fact software alone cannot provide compliance. Many of the requirements must be met procedurally, whilst other issues can be resolved by restricting access to the host PC, both physically and electronically.

You must also take into account that 21 CFR Part 11 is a document about electronic records and electronic signatures - it is not specific to the use of PCs and PC applications such as SCADA. As such, it is open to interpretation and its application is affected by guidance documents that are issued from time to time by the FDA.

21 CFR Part 11 contains subparts.

  • Subpart A - General Provisions. Describes the scope, implementation and definitions of terms used in 21 CFR Part 11. This subpart has no effect on the configuration of PcVue.
  • Subpart B - Electronic Records. Describes the controls for closed and open systems. PcVue is considered to be a closed system.
  • Subpart C - Electronic Signatures. Describes the implementation and use of electronic signatures. In PcVue, an electronic signature is known as a User Account.
