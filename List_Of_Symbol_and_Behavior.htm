List Of Symbol Animations and Behavior

 PcVue loads all the symbols used in a symbol animation when the container mimic is opened. There is the possibility of a performance penalty in the case of very complex symbols or animations such as symbol on Register-value or Text-value (exchange among multiple symbols).

Animation

Behavior

Bit

Selection of 2 symbols according to the status of a bit. A third symbol may be selected to represent the invalid state. Show picture

Bit group

Selection of up to 16 symbol according to the binary combination of up to 4 bits. An additional symbol may be specified that will be displayed if any of the bits are invalid. Show picture

Register bit

Selection of 2 symbols by a bit masked from a register using a binary rank. For example, if you use the 3rd bit, when the binary representation of the register is xxxxxxxxxxxx1xx the symbol selected for 1 will be displayed. When the binary representation of the register is xxxxxxxxxxxx0xx the symbol selected for 0 will be displayed (x = either 1 or 0). A third symbol may be selected to represent the invalid state. Show picture

Alarm

Selection of 4 symbols according to the 4 states of an alarm (Off, On - Nack, On - Ack, Off - Nack). A further 4 symbols may be selected to represent the various invalid conditions that an alarm may take. Show picture

Register value

Selection of 8 symbols according to up to 8 thresholds applied to a register. An additional symbol may be selected to represent the invalid state. See the section on changing color for an explanation of threshold operation. Show picture
Text value Selection of up to 32 symbols according to the equality of a text variable to 32 entered strings. A further three symbols can be selected according to the text variable being empty (null), not equal to any of the entered strings, or invalid. The animation is case sensitive or not depending on the value of the following Mimic Options: Enable case sensitivity for animations that compare text strings case sensitive. For example, if set for being case insensitive, HELLO, Hello, and hELLO would be treated as the same text value. Show picture
