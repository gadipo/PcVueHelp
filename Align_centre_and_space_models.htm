Aligning, Centering and Spacing 3D Models

When you use the alignment, centering or spacing tools, models are always moved along the 3D world axes that are parallel to the window's borders.

When they are displayed in a window using an orthographic camera mode, a 3D model's top, bottom, left and right sides are defined as follows.

Property Meaning

Side

Nearest to

Top side

The top border of window.

Bottom side

The bottom border of window.

Left side

The left border of window.

Right side

The right border of window.

The model's sides used for alignment, centering and spacing are the sides of a virtual rectangle (not displayed) that is just big enough to encompass the 2D projection of the model's bounding box onto the screen. When the HMI needs to calculate the center (e.g. for the centering tools) of a model, by 2D projection, it uses that virtual rectangle.

For example, in a window using the Top View orthographic camera mode, the 3D world's X and Z axes are the ones parallel to the window borders. Show picture

Alignment tools

Tool Icon Move the selected models

Align Left

Horizontally so that their left side aligns with the left side of the reference model.

Align Right

Horizontally so that their right side aligns with the right side of the reference model.

Align Top

Vertically so that their top side aligns with the top side of the reference model.

Align Bottom

Vertically so that their bottom side aligns with the bottom side of the reference model.

Align Right To Left

Horizontally so that their right side aligns with the left side of the reference model.

Align Left to Right

Horizontally so that their left side aligns with the right side of the reference model.

Align Bottom To Top

Vertically so that their bottom side aligns with the top side of the reference model.

Align Top To Bottom

Vertically so that their top side aligns with the bottom side of the reference model.
